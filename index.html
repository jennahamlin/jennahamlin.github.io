Jenna Hamlin is an evolutionary geneticist whose interests include comparative genomics, population genetics, sex and asex, speciation, and hybridization with respect to single and multi-ceullular organisms. Her work now is solely computationally based but she has spent bench-time narrowing genomic regions of interest in tomatoes.

Currently, she is working as a Post-Doctoral Researcher in the Bensassson Lab located in Athens, GA. You can find links to her associated publications at Google Scholar.

She can also be found commenting on science, healthcare, and re-tweeting animal pics on Twitter.

Jenna Hamlin is an evolutionary geneticist whose interests include comparative genomics, population genetics, sex and asex, speciation, and hybridization with respect to single and multi-cellular organisms. Her work now is solely computationally based but she has spent bench-time narrowing genomic regions of interest in tomatoes. Most recently, she developed a Rshiny application called tinselR for annotating outbreak trees, which is downloadable from GitHub.

Currently, she is working as a bioinformatician at CDC focused on understanding Legionella. You can find links to her associated publications at Google Scholar and a pdf version of her CV located here.

She can also be found commenting on science, healthcare, and re-tweeting animal pics on Twitter.
